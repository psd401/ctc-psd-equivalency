## Standards reference

Washington's HS Health credit (0.5 credit under the SBE 24-credit framework) must satisfy the OSPI 2016 K-12 Health Education Learning Standards, which adopt the 8 National Health Education Standards (concepts/disease prevention, analyzing influences, accessing valid info, interpersonal communication, decision-making, goal-setting, practicing health-enhancing behaviors, advocacy) organized into 6 Core Ideas: Wellness, Safety, Nutrition, Sexual Health, Social Emotional Health, and Substance Use and Abuse. The 6th-12th grade topic strands include Dimensions of Health/Disease Prevention/Hygiene, Injury Prevention/First Aid/Violence Prevention, Food Groups/Beverages/Label Literacy/Caloric Intake/Nutritional Planning, Anatomy-Reproduction-Pregnancy/Puberty/HIV-STI Prevention/Healthy Relationships/WA State Laws, Self-Esteem/Body Image-Eating Disorders/Stress Management/Emotions/HIB/Mental Health, and Substance Use-Effects-Prevention-Treatment-Legal Consequences. Several topics are legally REQUIRED by RCW: HIV/AIDS prevention (28A.230.070), comprehensive sexual health education (28A.300.475), mental health and suicide prevention (28A.230.095), CPR/AED instruction for HS students (28A.230.179), STD prevention/physiology/hygiene/effects of alcohol (28A.230.020), and sex trafficking awareness (28A.320.168). The standards focus on personal, family, and community health literacy through skill-building — NOT on professional/systems-level public health, healthcare administration, or clinical/research disciplines.

### Key K-12 Health strands
  - Wellness (Dimensions of Health, Hygiene, Disease Prevention, Analyzing Influences, Access Valid Information, Communication, Decision-Making, Goal-Setting)
  - Safety (Injury Prevention, First Aid, Violence Prevention, CPR/AED — RCW 28A.230.179)
  - Nutrition (Food Groups and Nutrients, Beverages, Label Literacy, Caloric Intake and Expenditure, Disease Prevention, Nutritional Planning)
  - Sexual Health (Anatomy/Reproduction/Pregnancy, Puberty and Development, Self-Identity, HIV/STI Prevention, Healthy Relationships, WA State Laws — Healthy Youth Act RCW 28A.300.475, sex trafficking awareness RCW 28A.320.168)
  - Social Emotional Health / Mental Health (Self-Esteem, Body Image and Eating Disorders, Stress Management, Expressing Emotions, Harassment/Intimidation/Bullying, Emotional and Mental/Behavioral Health, suicide prevention — RCW 28A.230.095)
  - Substance Use and Abuse (Use and Abuse, Effects, Prevention, Treatment, Legal Consequences — effects of alcohol/tobacco/drugs per RCW 28A.230.020)
  - Skill-based standards applied across all strands: analyzing influences (family/peers/media), accessing valid info/products/services, interpersonal communication, decision-making, goal-setting, health-enhancing behaviors, and advocacy for personal/family/community health

### Topics that misalign with K-12 Health
  - Epidemiology and disease surveillance (population-level study methods, not personal health behaviors)
  - Biostatistics and health research methods
  - Public health policy analysis, health systems financing, and health economics
  - Healthcare administration / health services management
  - Healthcare informatics, electronic health records, clinical data systems
  - Medical billing and coding, medical office administration, CPT/ICD coding
  - Medical terminology / anatomy and physiology taught as a clinical/CTE pre-professional course (belongs in CTE Health Sciences pathway, not the OSPI Health graduation strand)
  - Healthcare law, HIPAA compliance, medical ethics for practitioners
  - Environmental health policy, occupational health and safety regulation, industrial hygiene
  - Pharmacology, pathophysiology, clinical nursing/CNA prerequisites
  - Global health policy / international public health systems
  - Healthcare delivery systems, insurance and reimbursement
  - Community health worker / health promotion professional training (workforce-prep, not K-12 personal health literacy)
  - Medical sociology / health disparities research methodology
  - Nutrition science as a college-level biochemistry/dietetics course (vs. K-12 personal nutrition planning)
  - Exercise physiology and kinesiology at the college science level (these align with PE/Fitness credit, not Health)
  - Health education program planning and evaluation (professional/PHE-major coursework)

## ✅ Keep as Health (aligned)

- **cloverpark** `NUTR&101` Nutrition — Course directly addresses the Nutrition core idea of OSPI K-12 Health standards: macronutrients, micronutrients, food's role in growth/development, nutrition-related disease prevention, and developing a personal healthy dietary plan. The applied component (students create dietary plans for themselves and community members) aligns with personal nutritional planning, health-enhancing behaviors, and advocacy strands. While it includes anatomy/physiology of the GI tract (leaning college-level), the central focus on personal nutrition planning and disease prevention satisfies the Health Nutrition strand. Science (Non-Lab) appropriately captures the biological/physiological content.
- **pierce** `NUTR&101` Nutrition — Course directly addresses the K-12 Health Nutrition strand: food groups/nutrients, label literacy, caloric/energy balance, nutritional planning across the lifecycle, disease prevention from excesses/deficiencies, and analyzing influences (socioeconomic, cultural, media) on personal eating habits. Also covers body image and consumer literacy. This is personal nutrition literacy, not college-level biochemistry/dietetics professional training, so it aligns with OSPI Health standards. Retaining Science (Lab) as currently classified.
