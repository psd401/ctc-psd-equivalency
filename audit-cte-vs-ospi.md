# CTE classification audit vs OSPI K-12 standards

_877 of 877 courses audited._

## Summary

- **Keep CTE:** 805
- **Remove CTE:** 49
- **Add other credit type:** 23

## Standards reference

WA OSPI defines CTE as approved courses aligned to one of 16 national Career Clusters (Agriculture/Food/Natural Resources, Architecture/Construction, Arts/AV/Communications, Business/Management, Education/Training, Finance, Government/Public Admin, Health Science, Hospitality/Tourism, Human Services, IT, Law/Public Safety, Manufacturing, Marketing, STEM, Transportation/Distribution/Logistics) and grouped under five state program areas (Agricultural Education, Business & Marketing, Family & Consumer Sciences, Health Sciences, Skilled & Technical Sciences, plus STEM). Every approved CTE course must have a documented Curriculum Framework that ties content to current industry/nationally defined standards, Washington State CTE Program Standards, and aligned K-12 Learning Standards, with measurable performance assessments. Required components include integrated Leadership/Employability Skills (CTE Core Leadership Skills, Core Employability Skills, 21st Century Skills) applied in real-world business/industry contexts, with optional CTSO integration (DECA, FBLA, FCCLA, FFA, SkillsUSA, TSA) when all students participate. Courses must be either Exploratory (broad industry exposure) or Preparatory (occupation-specific) and address local skills-gap and advisory-committee input. To count for HS CTE credit under SBE's 24-credit framework, a community college course must align to one of these clusters AND deliver occupational/technical competencies plus leadership/employability skills, not just general academic content.

### Key strands
  - Career Cluster alignment (one of 16 national clusters) and OSPI program area (Ag Ed, Business & Marketing, FCS, Health Sciences, Skilled & Technical Sciences, STEM)
  - Industry/nationally-defined technical standards and competencies specific to the cluster (e.g., NATEF for auto, NCCER for construction, NAF for finance)
  - Washington State CTE Program Standards alignment documented in the Curriculum Framework
  - Leadership & Employability Skills integration (CTE Core Leadership Skills, Core Employability Skills, 21st Century Skills) applied in real-world contexts
  - Performance assessments: labs, projects, technical skill demonstrations (not solely exams/essays)
  - Exploratory vs Preparatory designation tied to CIP code and career pathway sequence
  - Optional but encouraged CTSO participation (DECA, FBLA, FCCLA, FFA, SkillsUSA, TSA) when all students engage
  - Skills-gap / workforce-need justification reviewed by local CTE advisory committee
  - Progression toward recognized postsecondary credential, certification, or apprenticeship
  - Academic rigor that may also satisfy core-subject equivalency (math, science, art, world language, ELA) when explicitly cross-walked

### Misfit topics
  - General-education humanities courses (e.g., ENGL& 101 English Composition, PHIL& 101 Intro to Philosophy, HIST& 146 US History) — academic transfer, no career cluster or industry standards
  - Pure academic math/science transfer courses without applied CTE framing (e.g., MATH& 141 Precalculus, BIOL& 211 Majors Biology, CHEM& 161) — fit Math/Lab Science credit, not CTE
  - Foreign language courses (e.g., SPAN& 121, FRCH& 121) — fit World Language credit, not CTE
  - Psychology/Sociology 101 academic-transfer courses without Human Services pathway scaffolding (no employability skills, no industry credential link)
  - Physical Education / activity courses (e.g., PE 101 Fitness, Yoga) — fit HS PE credit, not CTE Health Science / Skilled & Technical Sciences
  - Fine arts appreciation/history (e.g., ART 100 Art Appreciation, MUSC 105 Music Appreciation) — fit Fine Art credit, not CTE Arts/AV/Communications (which requires production/technical skills)
  - Communication Studies / Public Speaking (CMST& 220) when taught as general-ed speech without business/marketing or media-production cluster alignment
  - College success / student-success seminars (e.g., HD 101 College Survival, EDUC 100 College 101) — not aligned to any of the 16 clusters
  - Developmental / pre-college coursework (ABE, ESL, pre-100 level math/English) — not creditable toward HS graduation as CTE
  - Economics academic-transfer (ECON& 201/202) absent Finance or Business/Management pathway framing and industry standards
  - Intro to Education courses lacking PESB/Education & Training pathway alignment, advisory committee, and Educators Rising/CTSO linkage
  - Generic 'Intro to Computers' or keyboarding when not aligned to IT cluster industry certifications (CompTIA, Cisco, etc.)
